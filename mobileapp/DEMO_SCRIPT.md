# Water Tracker — 2‑Minute Demo Script

**Total: ~2 minutes** (adjust pace as needed)

---

## [0:00–0:20] Intro (20 sec)

> "This is Water Tracker — a simple Flutter app that helps you log how much water you drink each day.
>
> The daily goal is set to 2000 ml. The main screen shows today’s progress and a list of every time you added water."

*Show the home screen: progress card and empty or existing list.*

---

## [0:20–0:50] Adding water (30 sec)

> "To log water, I tap **Add water**.
>
> I choose an amount — for example 250 ml — and the app updates right away. The progress ring and the ‘X ml of 2000 ml’ text reflect the new total. Each entry appears in the list with the time.
>
> I’ll add a couple more so you can see the list and percentage change."

*Tap Add water → pick 250 ml, then 500 ml (or 200 ml). Point at the card and the list.*

---

## [0:50–1:15] Persistence (25 sec)

> "The app saves your data on the device. If I close the app and open it again, today’s total and the list of entries are still here — nothing is lost. The data resets automatically at the start of a new day."

*Optional: briefly close the app (or switch away) and reopen to show the same numbers and list.*

---

## [1:15–1:50] Summary & tech (35 sec)

> "So in short: you see daily progress, you add water with one tap and a choice of amounts, and everything is stored locally so it persists between sessions.
>
> It’s built with Flutter and uses SharedPreferences for simple local storage. The UI is basic Material Design — a single main screen and a dialog to add water — which keeps it clear for an assignment.
>
> That’s the demo. Any questions?"

---
